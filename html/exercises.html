Exercises

  • Box breathing is an exercise that slows the breathing and heart rate. It is commonly used to prevent panic attacks and involves a cycle of inhaling for four seconds, holding for four seconds, exhaling for four seconds, and holding for four seconds.

  • 4-7-8 breathing is a relaxation technique that helps reduce stress and anxiety. It involves inhaling for four seconds, holding the breath for seven seconds, and exhaling slowly for eight seconds to promote a sense of calm.

  • A mindful body scan is a meditation exercise that encourages awareness of physical sensations throughout the body. It helps individuals identify tension, improve focus, and develop mindfulness in the present moment.

  • The 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique is used to manage anxiety and overwhelming emotions by focusing attention on the present environment through the five senses.

  • Progressive muscle relaxation is an exercise that reduces physical tension by systematically tensing and relaxing different muscle groups. It can help decrease stress, improve relaxation, and increase awareness of bodily sensations.

  • Guided imagery is a relaxation technique that uses visualization to create calming mental images. It can help reduce stress, improve mood, and promote a sense of safety and well-being.
