Exercises

  • A structured breathing technique (4–4–4–4 pattern) that helps regulate the nervous system and reduce immediate stress or anxiety. Use it during moments of overwhelm, panic, or before high-pressure situations.

  • A slow, steady breathing exercise that lowers heart rate and promotes relaxation. Use it when you feel generally tense, restless, or need a mental reset during the day.

  • A guided attention exercise that moves awareness through the body to release tension and improve mind-body connection. Use it before sleep or when you feel physically or mentally disconnected.

  • A sensory awareness technique that brings attention to the present moment using sight, touch, and sound. Use it during anxiety, dissociation, or when you feel mentally overwhelmed.

  • A progressive tension-and-release method that reduces physical stress stored in the body. Use it after long periods of stress, studying, or physical fatigue.

  • A focus-based practice that trains attention and reduces mental clutter over time. Use it daily to build long-term emotional regulation and mental clarity.
